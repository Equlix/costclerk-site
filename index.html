How it works

Three steps. No app required.

CostClerk works entirely through email. Nothing to download, no dashboard to log into, and no manual data entry.

01

Forward your invoices

When you receive an invoice from a supplier (PDF, photo, or scan), just forward the email to your CostClerk address. That's the only action required on your end.

Supports PDF, PNG, JPG, WEBP, and TIFF up to 10MB.
02

AI reads every line item

CostClerk's engine extracts the supplier name, invoice number, every line item, amounts, and project references from the document. Even non-standard layouts are handled.

Each item is auto-categorised: materials, labour, transport, or other.
03

You get a cost breakdown

Within minutes, you receive an email with a CSV attachment. It's a clean, project-by-project summary showing where your money is going.

Open in Excel or Google Sheets. Columns: Project, Materials, Labour, Transport, Other, Total.

About

Why CostClerk exists

Contractors deal with dozens of invoices every week, from material suppliers, subcontractors, and transport providers. Most of them end up in an inbox or a shoebox. The actual cost picture only becomes clear at the end of the month when someone sits down with a spreadsheet and starts entering numbers by hand.

CostClerk was built to eliminate that manual work. Instead of tracking costs after the fact, contractors can forward invoices as they arrive and get an up-to-date breakdown in minutes, split by project and category, with every line item accounted for.

The system is deliberately simple. There's no software to install, no app to learn, and no dashboard to check. It works through email because that's where invoices already live. Forward the email, get the report.

What we believe
